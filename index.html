Five daily habits to keep your body toned
What to do if there is no time for sports at all
Not all people have the opportunity to go to the gym and work out with a personal trainer. But do not despair - you can be in good physical shape without tiring exercises.

1. Walking up the stairs

It's no secret that walking up the stairs is very beneficial not only for muscles, but also for the entire cardiovascular system.

2. Squats in the office.

Sitting at an office desk for hours is very harmful. Not to mention the discomfort. The Hollywood coach advises after an hour of work to take a few minutes for yourself and walk around the building or corridors. You can also lean your back against a wall, squat at a 90-degree angle, and hold that position for 60 seconds. This will help to disperse the blood in the numb limbs and keep the muscles in good shape.

3. Leave your gym bag in a convenient location.

To give yourself at least 20 extra minutes a day for training or jogging, he advises you to start spending your time more rationally. Derek says leaving your already assembled kit bag in the car, under your desk, or anywhere that's accessible and visible will help you find more time and force yourself to go to the gym.

4. The benefits of advertising.

If you are sitting at home watching TV, then a commercial break is a great reason to improve your health. Your coach advises you to spend these 3-5 minutes doing a great exercise called the plank. It will be most effective to do regular and lateral exercises for 45 seconds - 1 minute with short breaks in between.

5. Get a dog.

Or you can just pretend you have it. The main idea is to wake up a little earlier every day. You can just walk for 20-30 minutes before work, and you will be in a great mood together with a toned body.
