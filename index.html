Warm up and cool down for running
Training not only strengthens the muscles of the body, but also heals the entire body. But in order for a running, treadmill, or ellipse to only be beneficial, it is imperative to warm up before training and stretch after training.
During the warm-up, the players are psychologically tuned in to the training (match). Everyone has their own daily worries and problems, something that worries and worries. During training, especially with an important game, all this must be renounced, temporarily forgotten. Even a simple task cannot be solved if thoughts are constantly distracted by something else. It will not be possible to learn and master the feint, to outwit the opponent, etc. As a rule, no special exercises for the psychological mood are used during the warm-up. With its correct construction, the very process of starting training contributes to this.

Almost everyone knows about the importance of physical preparation for stress before the main part of training, otherwise the athlete will not be able to realize even half of his capabilities, or even get injured. The latter means a loss of physical fitness and skills acquired recently, as a result, less need of the player for the team.

Also, during the warm-up, the team interaction of athletes with each other is adjusted. It all starts with a regular run one after the other, ends with shots on goal. No matter how master each of the players is, the team cannot win without clear, coordinated interaction between the players.

If we are talking about away football matches, then during the warm-up it is important for athletes to adapt to a foreign field, the quality of its coverage, lighting, and climatic conditions. This happens throughout the entire workout, but the first stage is during the warm-up. During it, the athlete begins to feel the field, understand how to move more efficiently, adjust his breathing, etc.
Warm-up rules before starting a football game
The main ones are as follows:
  • Everything starts with the simplest, for example, jogging on the football field, and then gradually gets more complicated. It is clear that each organism is individual, but football is a group game, so a separate warm-up for each of the players is inappropriate. The only exception is the goalkeeper's warm-up, he trains according to a special program and is included in the general process only at the stage when shots on goal are practiced.
  • The warm-up program is designed in such a way that all players are in the right shape. By and large, this is the “weakest link rule”. If at least one of the players during the warm-up is not ready for training, then it will be less effective and the whole team will show the worst result.
  • In the morning, the warm-up takes longer because the body is more inhibited and relaxed after sleep. In the evening, the warm-up can be carried out in a truncated version. The body is pretty much prepared in a day.
Except for the very first rule, all others are not iron-clad. They need to be followed within the bounds of common sense. For example, if a player is injured, he trains and warms up according to a special program, omits some stages of the process, etc. Before the match, the warm-up can be more intense, regardless of the time of day.
Types of exercises for the warm-up of football players
The warm-up of football players consists of warm-up, warm-up of joints, stretching of muscles and ligaments, warm-up of the ankle and strikes on goal. Between the warm-up of the ankle and the shots on goal, you can do the running exercises again. There are, of course, other programs, but in most cases everything happens exactly according to this scheme. If necessary, if he considers it necessary, the coach stops at a different structure of warm-up sessions.

Warming up exercises
  • Light jogging for about 5 or 10 minutes. Usually they run from one end of the football field to the other, or along its perimeter.
  • Jogging exercise with a side step. It is familiar to many from school physical education lessons. Turn sideways, then send the first leg forward, put the second next to it. Drive in a straight line. First, the exercise should be done with the left side, then with the right.
  • Exercise running with a high rise of the thigh. When doing it, the knees are raised as high as possible to the chest. It is done on the spot or on the move.
  • Exercise running with a shin sweep. The heels touch the buttocks with each step. The body is slightly tilted forward, the arms move as in normal running.
  • Cross running exercise. You need to run not with a side step, but alternately crossing your legs. The body turns with each step in the opposite direction from the leading leg.
  • Hip swing exercise. Bend your knee, then make a semicircular motion with your hip.
  • Jumping exercise. It is rather difficult to explain the technique of performing this exercise in words. See how the high jumpers scatter.
